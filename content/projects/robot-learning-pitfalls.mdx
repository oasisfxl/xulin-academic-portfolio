## Why This Exists

Robot learning experiments often fail quietly. A model may train, logs may look stable, and evaluation may still be measuring the wrong thing. This note collects debugging patterns that are easy to forget when the pipeline becomes complex.

## Common Pitfalls

### Silent Dataset Bugs

Small convention mismatches can dominate the result: camera order, joint index order, gripper sign, episode boundaries, or timestamp alignment.

### Observation Leakage

If the policy receives information that will not exist at deployment time, the evaluation can look strong while the real robot fails immediately.

### Brittle Evaluation

Single-seed rollouts and cherry-picked demos are useful for debugging, but not enough for claims. Track distributions of failures, not only success examples.

<Callout title="Practical rule" type="warning">
Before tuning model size, first verify the data contract, action scale, normalization path, and evaluation reset conditions.
</Callout>
